Papel Bijoux

Small hand-made jewelry business

finds its home in the digital world

Context

Papel bijoux was born at the hand of Eliana Esquivel, an artisan who expresses and creates art through various forms. When she found herself out of work after a difficult time in her life, turning to her creations as a means of generating income was a natural choice. Each piece of jewelry is made entirely by hand through a painstaking process and Mrs. Esquivel mainly sells her products at artisan fairs and markets in different locations in the city.

The Challenge

Our client, Eliana, manifested an interest in digitizing the sale of her jewelry. She expressed the importance of growing her business and also diminishing the time she spends planning and attending artisan markets in order to have more time to invest in creating and selling her pieces from the comfort of her home.

Methodology

Defining the Problem

Mrs. Esquivel’s direct contact with her clients is mainly at the stand of the various artisan markets she attends. Her granddaughter, Catalina Cespedes, recently created an Instagram account for the jewelry business but due to how new the account is, it hasn’t gained much popularity as of yet.

During the interview conducted with both parties, we were made aware of the following:

  • Instagram account created less than a month ago
  • Account has very few followers
  • The account has not been reached by many
  • The only users who know of the account are acquaintances and family members

Benchmark

Due to the fact that our client already owned an Instagram account, we decided to start our research by investigating similar businesses within the same social media network. We realized early on that since the Papel Bijoux account had been created so recently, it lacked many of the elements that could potentially facilitate the growth and audience reach of the business. In addition, we investigated miscellaneous e-commerce websites that deal in similar products in order to draw inspiration and get a jump-start on the ideation of a useful digital marketplace.

Hypotheses

  • It is not easy for the user to attain detailed information about the business or its items for sale due to how recently the Instagram account was created.
  • The Instagram account doesn’t reach many followers; therefore, the only people who are aware of said account are acquaintances of Mrs. Esquivel or users who come in contact with the jewelry stand
  • The painstaking process of creating each piece of jewelry doesn’t translate through the account. It lacks photos that show the technique and the materials used.
  • We believe that if specific visual changes are made to the account and more information is presented, the account could reach a larger audience and increase sales.
  • If the quality of the materials used and the process of creation is presented, it will demonstrate the real value each piece of jewelry possesses.

Insights

For this process we took into account all the information we gathered during the beginning stages of our investigation, which gave us a foundation on which to base our ideas to resolve the issues at hand.

We believe that the following insights were the core of what would ultimately reflect the true value of this business, its artist and her creations.

Insights:

  1. There’s a very real desire for our client to create her jewelry that was born before it became a necessity to turn it into a business.
  2. It is of utmost importance to reflect the value of this business at its heart by showcasing our artist’s story, her design and handcrafting process, and the quality of the materials used.

How Might We?

As a result of the previous stage of defining the problem, we asked ourselves the following HMWs in order to continue into the process of ideation and generate concrete ideas to help resolve the issues found along the way and be able to present a useful final product.

  • How might we demonstrate our client’s love for handcrafting her jewelry?
  • How might we reflect the true value of this small business that comes from the extensive process of creation?

Brainstorming

Improvement ideas for instagram account
  • Upload photos showing our client handcrafting her jewelry pieces
  • Include payment and shipping information
  • Offer shipment to other regions in the country
  • Upload photos sent by satisfied customers wearing the jewelry
  • Upload photos of Eliana’s artisan stands
  • Convert account to business
  • Acquire better photos showing the real dimensions of each piece
  • Implement the use of hashtags
  • Announce the date and location of the artisan markets she will be attending in the future
Improvement ideas for physical artisan market stand
  • Obtain business cards
  • Tener más interacción con el usuario cuando se acerque al puesto. Mencionar los materiales usados en la elaboración del producto, lo duradero y original que es cada pieza, entregar tarjeta de presentación y comentar que pueden acceder a su página de Instagram
  • Posicionar en alguna parte del puesto el logo y el nombre del emprendimiento
Ideas to implement on website
  • Display Eliana’s short biography to create an emotional connection with the users
  • Description of the handcrafting process and the inspiration behind the desire to create this particular type of jewelry
  • Specify the materials used and the amount of time that was dedicated to making each piece
  • Make international shipping available as well as shipments to other regions within Chile
  • Display every product available for purchase

Sketching

As we began sketching our initial design, we decided to prioritize exhibiting our client’s personal story, the quality of the materials utilized, and the reflection of the origami tradition in Mrs. Esquivel’s products.

Mainly, our objective was to create a web page that was straightforward, easy to use, and that would effectively express what we wanted to portray: The artist’s story and process.

In order to achieve such venture, we created a section with a short biography of the artist that would be displayed on the right side of the page during every step of the navigation process. A longer version of this section would be available on the homepage of the website as a way for our audience to empathize with our client, engage on an emotional level, and encourage sales.

In addition to what was mentioned before, we also considered the desire our client expressed to migrate her sales to the digital world. Therefore, we created a navigation bar located at the top of the website which contained the various jewelry categories available for purchase (earrings, necklaces, collections, etc). Once a category was selected, an array of products would be displayed, each containing basic information such as the name of the piece, an image, and its price. A further selection of a specific item would disclose more images for further inspection, a small description of the item, the materials used, the price, and a purchase button. In case of a purchase, a shopping cart area was also made available to conclude the payment of the product.

Wireframe

Once we finalized the sketching process, we set out to produce wireframes to so that we could test our proposal with real users and confirm that what we wished to create would be truly useful. We implemented guerrilla usability testing to reach different user profiles and we received the following feedback:

  • Remove the artist’s profile once the shopping cart has been reached
  • Add a “Home” link to return to the initial page
  • Provide more information in the Upcoming Events section
  • Redesign the option we provided the user to receive the item gift wrapped
  • Add a modal to notify the user that the product was successfully added to the shopping cart
  • Provide shipping price in the shopping cart section

High Fidelity Prototype

In light of the feedback we received during the usability testing of our wireframe, we decided to implement some of those details in the high-fidelity design we created.

The visual design decisions were based on the existing logo we were provided by the client at the beginning of the project and on our desire to mirror the Japanese patterns from the materials used to make the jewelry. It was also important to maintain a minimalistic design in order efficiently highlight the product.

This final design was also tested and iterated until we achieved a product that would not only meet our client’s needs, but also that of her target users.

Conclusions

We came into this project without a clear vision of what we wished to develop. Papel Bijoux, being such a small business and having just begun flirting with the idea of exploring the digital space, left an enormous world of possibility.

Our first week was dedicated to research and understanding the artisan world in which our client made a living. During this time we noticed that this whimsical jewelry business lacked a few important details to thrive in the ecommerce arena but we also saw its tremendous potential, which ultimately became our driving force until the end.

Prior to developing our final product, we had considered implementing changes solely to our client’s Instagram page, but the information we gathered during our investigation pointed us in another direction.

We concluded that by creating an online marketplace for our client, she’d be able to smoothly transition her sales into the digital world and just as importantly, improve her quality of life.

It made us quite content to have the possibility of exposing the world to the handcrafted jewelry pieces made by a resilient and hardworking woman who so lovingly dedicates her life to bring art and uniqueness to our hectic lives.
